These instructions are tailored for my own ferris builds with different microcontrollers

For elite_pi and rp2040_ce
put each side of keyboard into boot mode, one at a time. Then, depending on which side is plugged in:

elte_pi:

qmk flash -c -kb ferris/sweep_mx_mbuk -km lindoman -e CONVERT_TO=elite_pi -bl uf2-split-left
qmk flash -c -kb ferris/sweep_mx_mbuk -km lindoman -e CONVERT_TO=elite_pi -bl uf2-split-right

rp2040_ce:
qmk flash -c -kb ferris/sweep_mx_mbuk -km lindoman-small -e CONVERT_TO=rp2040_ce -bl uf2-split-left
qmk flash -c -kb ferris/sweep_mx_mbuk -km lindoman-small -e CONVERT_TO=rp2040_ce -bl uf2-split-right


For pro-micro the controller can be flashed like this on one side, no need to set bootloader

pro-micro:
qmk flash -c -kb ferris/sweep_mx_mbuk -km lindoman-small
...and then like this for the other (optional)
qmk flash -kb ferris/sweep_mx_mbuk -km lindoman-small
